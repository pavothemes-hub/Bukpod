# Logo

This is WordPress's native **Site Identity** section - it sets the logo, site title/tagline, and browser favicon used across the whole site.

**Step 1** - Appearance >> Customize

<figure><img src="../.gitbook/assets/Screenshot 2024-07-31 at 10.15.01.png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

**Step 2:** Choose Site Identity

<figure><img src="../.gitbook/assets/image (369).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

**Step 3** - Under **Site Identity** you can set:

* **Logo** - upload your logo image. Bukpod's recommended size is **300 × 200px**, but the logo is flexible (any width/height ratio is accepted and scaled to fit).
* **Site Title** and **Tagline** - shown as text next to the logo when no logo is uploaded, and used by WordPress/SEO plugins for the site name and description.
* **Display Site Title & Tagline** - toggle this checkbox to show/hide the title and tagline text next to the logo.
* **Site Icon** - the favicon shown in the browser tab. Use a square image, at least 512 × 512px.

**Step 4** - Click **Publish** to save.

{% hint style="info" %}
Need a different logo on a specific Header template, or a separate logo for mobile/sticky header/transparent header? Use the **Bukpod Site Logo** Elementor widget instead of (or on top of) the Customizer logo - see [Elementor Widgets](../content-management/elementor-widgets.md). It lets you set a **Custom Image** per widget instance, independent from Site Identity.
{% endhint %}
